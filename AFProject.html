Analog Fuzz

This is a continuation of the Distortion Pedal project (found here)

The next interation had a visual schematic change (because the orginal was kinda ugly)

I also took out the star cut out because it didnt work. The idea was to use a LED as a diode and have it light up when playing, which would then shine through the board as light up the star. This didnt work which isnt surpising at all because there is not enough current to drive an led with how the circuit is set up. Cool idea though.

This interation had no room for the battery within the tin, the xx companonts where poking into the battery which can be a pick safety issue.

The next and current interation made room for the battery (which is under the analog fuzz logo), this is where this project got the analog fuzz name because it sounded cool. After some guitar player feedback I added some labels for the in and out because I found out that the standard is the other way in most guitar pedals for pedal board flow. I also made a small foot print error from the firtst ineration that I fixed. I also had feedback that the ODD AC logo thing was really hard to read.

This looked like drawing out the design on paper, life size and compariing it with batterys and companents to do the layout, putting that into photoshop and then adding an image into kicad on the front silk screen layer
